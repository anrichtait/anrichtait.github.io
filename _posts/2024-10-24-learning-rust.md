## **Introduction**
This project is heavily inspired by the limitations of classic game consoles, specifically the Pico-8 fantasy console. I will delve into the technical challenges, design decisions, and the lessons learned from working within a constrained environment, all while livestreaming the process on Twitch.

<!--page-->

## **The Vision**
The goal of this project is to build a fully functional game with the following constraints:
- **Resolution**: 128x128 pixels
- **Color Palette**: Limited to 16 colors
- **Sprites**: 256 8x8 pixel sprites
- **Map**: 128x32 tiles (expandable)
- **Sound**: Old-school 16-bit audio

These constraints will guide the design of both the gameplay and art, forcing me to focus on simplicity and creativity. The final result should feel like a modern take on retro-style games, with smooth mechanics and pixel art that evokes the nostalgia of early game consoles.

## **Inspiration**
The inspiration for this project comes from the minimalist design philosophy behind Pico-8, a fantasy console known for its highly constrained environment. Games like *Celeste* (the original Pico-8 version) show how creativity thrives within limitations. By adhering to a fixed resolution and color palette, these games focus on core gameplay mechanics and elegant design, which is what I hope to replicate in my own game.

Additionally, this project serves as a way for me to learn Rust, a powerful systems programming language, while experimenting with **Macroquad**, a game engine that offers simplicity and cross-platform support.

<!--page-->

## **Twitch / Streaming**
As part of this project, I will be livestreaming the development process daily on Twitch. These streams will cover everything from learning Rust, building game mechanics, designing pixel art, and troubleshooting challenges along the way. My hope is to create an interactive learning experience where viewers can see the game take shape in real time, ask questions, and offer feedback.

I will post regular updates here and on my Twitch channel, where you can follow the project's progress and join me on this technical exploration of retro game development.
